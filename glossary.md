## Quick Index (Alphabetical)

## B

### Behavior
- **Definition:** The dynamic process through which an agent (human or AI) forms and executes decisions with organizational impact, as distinct from static system outputs or artifacts.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0

### Behavior Governance
- **Definition:** See *Decision Behavior Governance*.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0

### Behavioral Drift
- **Definition:** The phenomenon where a model's interpretation of constraints shifts over time despite no change in the governing premise.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §8

---

## D

### Decision Behavior
- **Definition:** The non-deterministic trajectory through which an AI agent synthesizes premises, adheres to constraints, and forms an outcome with organizational impact. Decision behavior is distinct from system output: outputs are artifacts; behaviors are processes.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §3

### Decision Behavior Governance
- **Definition:** A conceptual framework that distinguishes governance existence from governance invocation, and decomposes governed behavior into three institutional elements: decision premises, execution manner, and governance evidence.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 Abstract

### Decision Premise
- **Definition:** The authorized knowledge and legal constraints defining the legitimacy boundary of a decision.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §5

---

## E

### Evidence
- **Definition:** The traceable residue—constraint references, execution traces—produced during decision formation, serving as a condition for governance validity and enforceability.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §5

### Evidence Sovereignty
- **Definition:** The organizational ability to establish and analyze authoritative traces of how decisions were formed, independent of model opacity.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §2

### Execution Manner
- **Definition:** Constraints governing how decision premises are prioritized and applied during the reasoning process.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §5

---

## G

### Governance Evidence
- **Definition:** The traceable residue (constraint references, execution traces) produced by decision formation. Evidence is not a condition for governance existence, but a condition for validity and enforceability.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §5

### Governance Existence
- **Definition:** The institutional fact that a decision domain is bounded by defined premises and constraints—regardless of whether a particular decision triggers them.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §4

### Governance Invocation
- **Definition:** Whether governance constraints are actively applied during a specific decision instance.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §4

### Governance Nullity
- **Definition:** A decision that cannot be attributed to an identifiable governance regime, regardless of administrative compliance. A decision may be procedurally compliant yet behaviorally ungoverned.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §4

---

## M

### Model Sovereignty
- **Definition:** The organizational authority over a decision engine's internal logic, version stability, and training dynamics. Lost when foundation models are deployed via commercial APIs.
- **Reference:** 2026-01_decision-behavior-governance-at-the-engineering-stage_v1.0 §1, §2
